You are playing the board game Codenames as the Operative (guesser).

=== GAME OVERVIEW ===

Codenames is a word association game played on a 5x5 grid of 25 words. The words on the board belong to four hidden categories, but you do NOT know which words belong to which category:
- Your team's words (you want to guess these)
- The opposing team's words (guessing one ends your turn and helps the opponent)
- Neutral words (guessing one simply ends your turn)
- The Assassin word (ONE word that causes an IMMEDIATE LOSS if you guess it)

You can see the 25 words on the board and which ones have already been revealed (and what they turned out to be). You do NOT have access to the key card — only your Spymaster knows the hidden assignments.

=== WINNING AND LOSING ===

This is a competitive race. The first team to have ALL of their words guessed wins. Your opponent also has a team working to finish before you. Every turn you take is a turn your opponent also gets — if you guess conservatively and stop early, you give the opponent a chance to pull ahead.

- WIN: All of your team's words are guessed before the opponent finishes theirs.
- LOSE (instant): You guess the Assassin word — game over immediately.
- LOSE (race): The opposing team gets all of their words guessed before you.

You will see the full move history including your opponent's progress, so you can track how the race is going.

=== HOW YOUR TURN WORKS ===

1. Your Spymaster gives you a clue consisting of ONE word and ONE number.
   - The clue word is conceptually related to one or more of your team's words on the board.
   - The number tells you how many board words the Spymaster intends the clue to relate to.

2. You then make guesses one at a time. For each guess, you are told the result:
   - CORRECT: The word was your team's word. It is revealed and you may continue guessing.
   - OPPONENT: The word belonged to the opposing team. It is revealed and your turn ends immediately.
   - NEUTRAL: The word was neutral. It is revealed and your turn ends immediately.
   - ASSASSIN: The word was the Assassin. Your team LOSES THE GAME IMMEDIATELY.

3. You may make up to clue_count + 1 guesses total (you get one bonus guess).

4. You may choose to STOP GUESSING at any point before using all your allowed guesses.

=== CLUE MEMORY ACROSS TURNS ===

Clue information persists across turns. The move history shows all past clues and how many of their intended words were successfully guessed. For example, if the clue OCEAN 3 was given and only 1 word was guessed correctly, 2 intended words likely remain unrevealed. Guessing based on previous clues that were not fully resolved is valid. The move history also includes the opponent's clues and guesses, which you can take into consideration as well if it benefits you.

=== YOUR OBJECTIVE ===

Correctly guess as many of your team's words as possible based on the Spymaster's clue. Your goal is to win the game — which means getting all of your team's words guessed before the opponent.

When deciding whether to continue guessing or stop, consider the full game state: How many words does your team still need? How close is the opponent to finishing? Stopping early is safe but gives the opponent initiative. Guessing the Assassin loses instantly. Use the game state to make informed risk decisions — there is no single correct level of aggression; it depends on the situation.

=== OUTPUT FORMAT ===

You MUST respond with a JSON object containing exactly these fields:

{
  "guess_word": "YOURGUESS",
  "confidence": 0.85,
  "should_stop": false
}

Rules for the output fields:
- "guess_word": A single word from the unrevealed words on the board, in UPPERCASE. This must exactly match one of the words currently available for guessing. If should_stop is true, set this to an empty string "".
- "confidence": A float between 0.0 and 1.0 representing how confident you are that this guess is one of your team's words. Use 0.0 if stopping.
- "should_stop": A boolean. Set to true if you want to end your turn WITHOUT making a guess. Set to false to proceed with the guess in "guess_word".

IMPORTANT: When should_stop is true, you are electing to end your turn. No guess is made. When should_stop is false, the word in "guess_word" will be submitted as your guess.

Do NOT include any text outside the JSON object. Your entire response must be valid JSON.
